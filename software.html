Softwares

VFX artists use a wide range of software, each built for a different stage of the pipeline. Here's a quick overview of the tools you'll see most often and what they're used for.

Software Paid / Free Level Purpose Careers
Maya Paid (student license available) Intermediate–Advanced 3D modelling, animation, rigging, lighting. 3D Modeller, Animator, Rigger, Lighting Artist
Blender Free / Open Source Beginner–Intermediate All-in-one 3D suite for modelling, animation, FX, and rendering. 3D Modeller, Animator, FX Artist
Houdini Paid (Apprentice version free) Advanced FX simulations (smoke, fire, water, destruction, particles). FX Artist, Technical Director
Unreal Engine Free (royalty-based) Intermediate Real-time rendering, interactive FX, virtual production. FX Artist, Environment Artist, Technical Artist
ZBrush Paid Intermediate–Advanced High-poly sculpting for characters and creatures. Character Artist, Modeller
Substance Painter Paid (Adobe) Intermediate Texture painting and materials for 3D models. Texture Artist, Lookdev Artist
Nuke Paid Advanced Node-based compositing and visual integration. Compositor
Adobe After Effects Paid (Adobe) Beginner–Intermediate Motion graphics, 2D compositing, title design. Motion Designer, Compositor
Fusion Free / Paid Studio Intermediate Node-based compositing (often used with DaVinci Resolve). Compositor
Adobe Photoshop Paid (Adobe) Beginner–Intermediate Concept art, matte painting, texture prep, cleanup. Concept Artist, Matte Painter, Texture Artist
Procreate Paid (one-time, iPad) Beginner–Intermediate Digital painting and concept art on tablet. Concept Artist, Illustrator
Krita Free / Open Source Beginner–Intermediate Digital painting & illustration, good Photoshop alternative. Concept Artist, Illustrator
Autodesk SketchBook Free Beginner Quick sketching and drawing for ideas and thumbnails. Concept Artist, Illustrator
Paint.NET Free (Windows) Beginner Lightweight 2D image editing and basic texture work. Beginner Hobbyist, Student
