The Pipeline

The VFX pipeline is the step-by-step process used to create visual effects for films, games, and animation. Each stage focuses on a different part of the work — from planning ideas, to creating 3D assets, to adding effects and final polish.

Pre-Production

Concept Design

Early sketches and ideas that define the look, mood, and style of the project.

Storyboarding

Visualizing the script shot-by-shot to plan camera angles, action, and timing.

Previsualization

Rough 3D blocking used to test composition, pacing, and camera movement.

Rigging

Building digital skeletons and controls so characters can move later.

Production

Modelling

Creating 3D characters, props, and environments based on the concepts.

Animation

Bringing characters and objects to life with movement.

Camera Tracking

Matching real camera motion to 3D so digital elements fit the filmed footage.

Rendering

Simulating light, shadow, and reflections, then generating final image sequences.

Post-Production

FX Simulation

Creating effects like smoke, fire, water, particles, and destruction.

Compositing

Combining 3D renders and filmed footage into a seamless final image.

Colour Grading

Adjusting color, exposure, and tone so the entire project feels unified.
